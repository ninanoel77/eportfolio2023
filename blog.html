Coffee and Comments

By: Nina Noel

Good morning to all my caffeine lovers out there! Today we will be discussing the best coffee shops around Tampa Bay, in my opinion, of course. Ever since coming to Tampa as a college student three years ago, I have tried more coffee shops and cafes than I can count. I truly don’t think I ever tried one I didn’t like. Some people say a store-bought coffee is a waste of money – but you know what I say to that? I say if a five-dollar cup of coffee makes your day a little brighter, it’s definitely worth the money. A cup of joe in the morning as I start my day always makes the tasks ahead a little easier. That is why I love cafes so much! Waking up in the morning, in the sunshine state and having my toughest decision of the day being where to get my iced coffee. Walking to class with a cup of happiness gets me started on the right foot. Not only is coffee delicious and makes me nice and awake, but the act of getting coffee is just so relaxing. So, let's roll our windows down and cruise through my favorite spots in town.

Buddy Brew Nation

Buddy Brew coffee is probably one of Tampa Bay’s top coffee spots. This coffee is so adored that coffee shops around Tampa secretly use their brew! Originally located in Hyde Park and flooding outwards, this place is beloved to locals and tourists.

Personally, I am a big cold brew lover. Honestly, it's my go-to. Buddy Brew has set the standard for cold brew coffee but creating probably the strongest brew I have ever tried. My personal drink order is a Cold Brew Float, which is Buddy Brew’s play on a “root beer float.” This amazing drink is a plain ole’ cold brew with a huge scoop of vanilla gelato inside. Now, I only have this on special occasions because I am sure the sugar content is through the roof, but it is truly one of my faves. I don’t know why I never thought of this! Not to mention, this delicious gelato cold brew makes it so the customer does not need any ice in their coffee, saving you from a watered-down brew (AKA the worst).

King State in the State of Florida 

King State was brought to my attention through my very own sister. As she lived in Seminole Heights last year, she came across this hidden gem. Originally, it was an auto shop/gas station! From the outside, it does still resemble its original structure. Somehow, these owners made this place into an incredible hipster hangout where anyone is welcome. This Cuban coffee shop has cold brew on tap! My personal favorite has got to be the Café Con Leche. This amazing coffee can be ordered hot or iced - but you know me, iced all the way. This shot of strong espresso is topped with steamed milk and any flavoring of your choice. They even have a million milk options for you lactose-intolerant folk. I always need a bit of vanilla flavoring, or seasonal flavoring to finish it off. Or go bold and drink it as is!

Now for the best part. This place turns into a BAR at night. Yes, a bar with alcohol and coffee. Of course, I had to get the espresso martini, duh! It was amazing. And to top it off, there was even a talent show when I went. I got to sit back and sip on my martini as I watched people sing, freestyle, dance, and more. The vibes definitely changed with the time of day. During the day, this place was well-lit, open, and overall felt like a coffee shop. But at night, they close all the windows with black-out curtains, turn on low-lights, play jazz music, and switch from coffee to vodka!

P.S. Their food was pretty good too...

Oxford Exchange

Oxford Exchange is not just a cafe, but a whole restaurant, library, gift shop, and eye-glass store, all in one. When you walk into this adorable yet sophisticated restaurant, you will want to live there. The ambiance in this place is fantastic. There is even a huge water fountain in the main dining room? Also, I am a sucker for gift shops, especially when they are at the main entrance. How can I not buy a nick-nack to remember this coffee run?

Nevertheless, the brunch food is amazing, the coffee is amazing, and the dinner is amazing. This restaurant is one of the most popular spots in Tampa. The service is a 10/10 and they even offer Buddy Brew coffee beans here! Usually, if I am here, it is for the food mainly, but after brunch or dinner, I always enjoy a little something sweet. For dessert, I like to order the OE espresso, which is similar to a macchiato, but rather than the espresso being mixed with just milk, this espresso is topped with a velvety milk foam. On the side, they give customers a sugar crystal mixer to mix into your hot espresso and it liquifies the sugar, so you don’t get any sugar grains when drinking.

Blind Tiger

This café is very similar to Buddy Brew. Taking off in Ybor City, Tampa, this café has also slowly taken over the Bay with their brew. This rustic café has such diverse options, not only just for coffee, but for other drinks as well. Nevertheless, I still always get iced coffee, but they have plenty of lemonades and teas for those who may not want coffee. The last time I went here was the beginning of the fall season, so you know I had to get a seasonal autumn flavor. This time, I went with one I had never tried before, the iced Tiger Spice latte. Normally, I don’t go for lattes just because they are too milky for me, but this latte was to-die-for. With cinnamon and nutmeg undertones, I felt like walking through a pumpkin patch and making an apple pie. My friend had gotten the lilac lemonade, and it was also phenomenal.

This cute local café had tons of sweet treats for customers to choose from, but since I had arrived after their scheduled breakfast time, I decided to just get one of their many flavors of macaroons. From fruit loops, to vanilla, to mint-chocolate, they had lots to choose from. These macaroons were refrigerated and for some reason, that made them so much better. I will never eat a warm macaroon again!

Cooking Up in the Lab: The Lab Coffee

The Lab Coffee is fairly new, and I have only recently learned of its existence. Located in North Hyde Park, this café was directly behind my old apartment complex, and I never knew if it was good until I finally built up the courage to go there and risk my dollars. I was pleasantly surprised by this café's environment and their fun twists on regular coffee orders. Like I have said before, cold brew is my go-to, but can you believe this place has a fruity pebble cold brew? I couldn’t. When I tell you, my heart skipped a beat – I mean it. This coffee was absolutely electric. Next time I go, I am going to have to try the “caramel crunch latte,” because I could smell this latte being created from across the room. I don’t even want to know the sugar content in these, but trust me, it was good. On the side, I had to get a Nutella croissant. Obviously, the Nutella inside was amazing, but this flakey croissant was out of this world! It is a must-try if you enter this lab.

Coffee Connoisseurs Unite!

Honestly, I could go on and on, but I’ll stop here. Whether you’re a coffee-lover or a tea-drinker, these cafes offer more than just caffeine. They offer an experience! Every single one of these cafes I have listed have an unbeatable ambiance to them that I can’t even explain. So go relax, read a book, maybe get some studying done, and grab whatever beverage you like at one of these amazing places here in Tampa Bay.
